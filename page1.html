 There is a popular activity within the Grand Theft Auto franchise where the player fires a rocket into a crowded square just to see how long they can evade the cops. With magic, the player is the rocket launcher. The player’s innate arsenal gives them complete power over the simulated citizens.

 Diamond Leadership writes that power over others leads to disinhibition and does serve a purpose in real life. In video games, however, disinhibition serves the purpose of freeing the player from societal constraints. However, unlike with drunkenness or other kinds of games, video games are a private experience. Magic heightens this experience by allowing the player vents that would be unavailable in any other genre of game. The player could collapse buildings, turn civilians into landmines, or write expletives on the moon, and then reset the game and no damage is done.

 There is a debate as to whether games like this cross the line from stress or rage management to sadism; however, given the fantasy of these games, they are often, “purposefully abstracted to discourage you from thinking of the violence in realistic terms”(Portnow 4:26). To the player of these games, the characters are more like the buildings than the people. Though in games like Fictorum, they are like buildings with snipers in them.

 Fictorum is based on freely destructible buildings and mid game spell customization. The buildings add satisfaction to the spell slinging and can allow the player to make self-imposed challenges. But spells themselves have up to three modifiers that can be changed in the middle of combat. Whenever the player casts a spell, they can add varying degrees of each modifier. The more of each they add the more expensive and slow the spell is, but modifiers include multi-shot, range, and explosiveness among others. This system dramatically increases the range of what the player can do and makes them feel as if they are shaping the spells as they cast them, further contributing to the feeling of power. These games make the player feel as if they can do almost anything for a couple hours with the only consequence being to their time investment.
