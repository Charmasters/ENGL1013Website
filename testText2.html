About

(classracegender.wordpress.com)
The United States of America is a country that is known for the waves of immigrants that have come in over the centuries, making it one of the most diverse countries in the world. People come here to find freedom and opportunities they could not find back in their homelands. All Americans are racially, culturally, and linguistically different, but many Americans grow up being bilingual through different schooling methods.

As years progress, the number of immigrants in the country continues to grow, totaling up to 41.3 millions or 13.1 percent of the American population in 2013, according to the Migration Information Source. With the increasing number of immigrants, many choose to live in areas with people who speak the same native language, thus they could live in this country without ever fully becoming fluent in English. From the Language Use in the United States: 2011 report, 58.2% of people who speak other languages at home claim that they can speak English "very well," which is only a little over half of the population. This statistic is what is concerning to many English-speaking Americans.

Language barrier between English-speaking and limited-English-proficient (LEP) people is what is preventing Americans from having complete trust in each other, and from communicating at work effectively. Teaching immigrant children English seems to be the best solution to break the barrier. Fluency in English among immigrants will ensue that tasks at work are carried efficiently by peers who speak different languages, and will promote social harmony among culturally-diverse Americans. Which is the best method to teach English to immigrants?
