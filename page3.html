 Discovery must come before creativity and power in a magic system. Players choose to discover not just because they are curious, but because the knowledge can strengthen their magic in innovative ways. Curiosity is still important to designers if they want players to explore. Unfortunately, this means predicting the psyche of their notoriously fickle players.

 To help understand the nature of curiosity, Celeste Kidd and Benjamin Hayden wrote a paper on the subject. They reference a study which correlated a high degree of curiosity with having “some idea about the answer, but lack[ing] confidence”(Kidd and Hayden).

 To a game designer, this suggests that they need to properly explain their system with a tutorial, an intuitive user interface, or some other method which allows the player to understand the basics. However, the player will not try to learn more if they feel like they know everything. Beyond balancing confusion, curiosity, and boredom for studious players, the developer also needs to engage less focused players.

 Kid and Hayden recorded another study which tested how well subjects recalled pictures of faces both during and outside of answering trivia. Those who were engaged in trivia could also recall the faces more accurately suggesting that, “the curiosity state led to better learning, even for the things people weren’t curious about” (Kidd and Hayden).

 This means that by creating lore, a world, and characters that interest the player, the player will be able to remember other aspects of the game as well. On top of the story and world, the role playing games in the Ultima Underworld series use a system of rune crafting to encourage experimentation. As the player gains new runes, they can try them with other runes to learn spells. This directly translates to a spell list, however, and being the only arcane experimentation, can become tedious.

 Another game to attempt discovery is UnReal World. It is a massive and feature rich fictionalization of ancient Finland and is not purely a fantasy game. What magic it does have is mysterious and ties to the world and the atmosphere. For example, the player may hear from a character that kicking a tree with their heel prevents squirrels from leaving it. In game mechanics, this translates to approaching a tree, facing away from it, and kicking the tree to make hunting the furry rodents easier.

 Few games attempt discovery in their magic systems due to the difficulty of making an intricate ‘system’ for their magic, but the extra depth will appeal to a niche of players who want to feel truly like Gandalf.
